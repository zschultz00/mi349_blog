Undergrad Research at SPARTIE Lab

Written by Zachary Schultz
Published 10-13-2022

After Zachary Schultz transferred to Michigan State University (MSU) from Oakland University, he got involved with research at SPARTIE Lab. On one of his first days as a transfer student, Zachary was sitting in MI 101. Dr. Ratan, the professor for MI 101, mentioned his podcast. After class Zachary went on YouTube to listen to some episodes of the podcast and he was immediately interested in the virtual reality research Dr. Ratan conducts. Fast forward and Zachary began getting involved doing research within SPARTIE Lab, Dr. Ratan’s lab. Zachary quickly became immersed in cutting edge virtual reality research. He worked with other undergraduate students assisting graduate students to develop a simulation for an experiment using Unity. Zachary and some of the other undergraduates wanted to share the super interesting research happening at SPARTIE Lab. MSU has a forum for undergraduates to share their research called University Undergraduate Research and Arts Forum (UURAF). Zachary designed a poster to describe the virtual reality experiment and the development tools the group uses. Zachary also took on an independent side research project looking into existing research regarding potential health risks associated virtual reality technologies. He turned his findings another poster. Zachary presented both posters at UURAF. As of October 2022, one of his research posters is on display within the College of Communication Arts and Sciences building at MSU. Zachary is still a member of SPARTIE Lab engaging with Virtual Reality research.

Spartan Linux Users Group (SLUG)

Written by Zachary Schultz
Published 10-12-2023

Zachary cofounded Spartan Linux Users Group (SLUG) Fall semester 2021. Zachary and his peers gathered due to their shared interest of Linux. The group quickly registered as a student organization at Michigan State University (MSU). Due to the club forming in the middle of a semester membership was slow to grow. Eventually the club began to have more members join after Zachary and another member of the eBoard began promoting the club. The club was promoted with poster spread across MSU’s campus and at a student club event called Sparticipation. At Sparticipation Zachary engaged with many MSU students telling them about all of the exciting things the club does. SLUG’s main project has been establishing mirrors of popular Linux distributions (e.g., Arch Linux, Debian, and Ubuntu). The club is working to get the mirrors officially recognized. In addition to their mirrors, the club meets every other week to have fun discussing Linux related news. We also enjoy sharing our knowledge with each other and our personal Linux projects. As gaming on Linux becomes more common due to the Steam Deck, made by Valve, the club has been sharing excited to discuss how to get the best gaming experience on Linux. Information about SLUG can be found at https://mirrors.egr.msu.edu.
